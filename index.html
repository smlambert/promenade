About the Planting Site

The Promenade at Miner's Point is a community orchard located along the scenic waterfront walkway on the eastern edge of the park. The site features 30 fruit and nut trees arranged in six planting beds separated by the main promenade path.

Trees were selected for their suitability to the coastal Bay Area microclimate and their value to the community—providing seasonal fruit, habitat for pollinators, and a living demonstration of urban food forestry. Planting, maintenance, and harvest are carried out by community volunteers.

Growing Notes

  • Climate: The site sits in USDA Zone 10a. Mild, frost-free winters and cool, foggy summers favour stone fruits, figs, and citrus.
  • Irrigation: Drip irrigation is installed on all six beds. During dry season (May–October) trees receive weekly deep watering.
  • Pollination: Most stone fruit varieties are at least partially self-fertile; additional pollinator varieties have been paired within each bed to maximise set.
  • Pruning: Annual winter pruning workshops are held in January. Volunteers are welcome — contact the site steward to join.
  • Harvest sharing: Ripe fruit is available to any visitor. Surplus harvest is donated to the neighbourhood food pantry.
  • Pests & disease: Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices are used exclusively. No synthetic pesticides are applied on site.
